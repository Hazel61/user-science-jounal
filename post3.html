User Testing Sea Wolf Bakery's Website

Posted by Shannon Michaels on April 20, 2019

For my third post I want to write about the importance of user testing and provide some examples from my own data. I think it is reasonable to expect that websites for businesses will include a description of their product, hours of operation and location, and these features should be prominent without the need for too much scrolling or searching. I feel restaurants have the additional requirement of including mouthwatering pictures of their product as soon as possible on their main page.

User testing allows us to test our intuition about the major problems with a website by having other eyes review it. It is helpful to include a wide demographic of age, computer ability, and gender in your testing pool, and I guarantee you will find additional user issues in the process.

The website I reviewed is the main site for Sea Wolf Bakers, a Seattle Bakery so popular that there is a line that snakes out the door on Saturday morning. In full disclosure, I have been in that line repeatedly, as I love their cinnamon rolls. The homepage for the bakery is shown below.

Seawolfbakers.com

My First Impressions

After spending some time on the site reviewing its visual appeal, architecture, and components, my overall impression is positive. My findings are summarized below.

My Impressions

  1. The main page has a logo, the bakery's name, and a nav bar where a viewer would expect them to be.
  2. Account and login utilities are not present but not necessary
  3. A search option is missing and would be useful.
  4. Is it easy to back up or go home?
  5. Where is the search button?
